    Product Requirements Document

    Team Assignment

    Introduction

    Product requirements documents are one way to develop, rationalize, and specify in engineering terms the requirements of your product. Developing product requirements comes after identifying user needs, where an engineering team converts what they heard from the user into a set of engineering requirements that follow the SMART rule: Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, and Time-bound.

    This assignment asks you to read more on product requirements documents and for your team to create its own. Pay special attention to the references to decision matrices, Quality Function Deployment (QFD), and other tools with more information linked below.

    Instructions

    1. Read this article on Product Requirements Documents.

    2. Create a copy of the template provided, and develop your own product requirements document.

    3. Include all sections provided by the template up to but not including the Appendices.

      1. Include at least two use cases, from the perspectives of how users interact with the product in either 1) two particular types of venues, 2) two different interaction examples, or 3) two different stakeholders that interact with the product (e.g. patient and therapist).

      2. Include the following design aspects:

        • Hardware / Product Design (the latter of which will be outsourced to an external designer)
        • Software / Functionality
        • Interactivity & User Experience
        • Customization
        • Manufacturing
        • Safety (Feel free to include any safety regulations you come across in your research)

    Most Common Mistakes

    1. Writing performance specifications that are not measurable. If a criterion is not measurable (e.g., "soft") how will you know whether or not you were successful? Research appropriate ways to measure each of your specifications to precisely define success.

    2. Poorly formatting specifications such that tables span across many pages.
